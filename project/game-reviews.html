Game Reviews

Battlefield 4

Battlefield 4 is, without a doubt, one of the most iconic and enduring first-person shooters ever made. Released in 2013, it still outshines most modern FPS games in terms of depth, gameplay, and sheer fun factor. I’ve spent countless hours playing with friends, and even today, I keep coming back for more.

The game’s hallmark is its massive, chaotic multiplayer battles. The 64-player matches on sprawling maps make every match feel like a unique war story. Whether you're parachuting onto a contested rooftop, engaging in intense tank battles, or blowing up entire buildings to flush out enemies, Battlefield 4’s emphasis on teamwork and strategy sets it apart from the run-and-gun style of many competitors.

Despite being over a decade old, its visuals and sound design remain surprisingly sharp. The attention to detail in explosions, weapon sounds, and the dynamic weather effects immerses you like few games can. And the gameplay? Still as addictive as ever. The gunplay feels tight and satisfying, and the sheer variety of vehicles—jets, helicopters, boats, tanks—offers endless opportunities for experimentation.

What makes Battlefield 4 truly special is how it brings people together. Playing with friends, coordinating over voice chat, and pulling off ridiculous stunts (like C4-ing a tank or roadkilling someone with a jet) creates memories that stick with you. Few games capture that balance of chaos and camaraderie so well.

Even with modern FPS games boasting better graphics or new mechanics, Battlefield 4's quality remains unmatched. Its focus on large-scale, team-based combat with a sprinkle of destruction makes it timeless. If you're nostalgic for the golden age of FPS games or looking for a shooter that’s still alive and kicking, Battlefield 4 is an absolute gem.

Galaga

Galaga is the definition of a classic. Simple yet addictive, it’s an arcade shooter that has stood the test of time for good reason. Piloting a tiny spaceship, you fend off relentless waves of alien enemies in a challenge that’s easy to pick up but tough to master. It’s a game that’s as nostalgic as it is timeless, capturing the magic of arcade gaming in every pixel and beep.

One of Galaga’s standout features is its simplicity. There’s no fluff—just you, your ship, and a screen full of enemies. But beneath that simplicity lies a surprising depth. Learning to dodge enemy fire, strategize against swooping alien formations, and maximize points by rescuing your captured ship creates a level of engagement that few games can match, even today.

The sound effects are iconic. From the satisfying hum of your blasters to the unmistakable “whoosh” of alien ships diving toward you, the audio is a core part of Galaga’s charm. It’s the kind of soundscape that instantly transports you back in time, even if, like me, you weren’t alive when it first released.

Playing Galaga on an emulator is fun, but nothing compares to the real arcade experience. There’s something about standing at the cabinet, gripping the joystick, and hearing the unmistakable click of the buttons as you fight to climb the leaderboard. It’s pure nostalgia—a throwback to an era when gaming was about skill, focus, and a pocket full of quarters.

Galaga is a game that transcends generations. Whether you’re discovering it for the first time or revisiting it decades later, it delivers the same heart-pounding excitement. It’s a testament to the fact that great gameplay doesn’t need flashy graphics or complex mechanics—it just needs to be fun. And Galaga is endlessly fun.

God of War

God of War (2018) is a breathtaking reimagining of a legendary series, and it’s easily one of the greatest single-player experiences I’ve ever had. Having grown up playing the classic God of War games, I loved seeing Kratos evolve into a more nuanced and layered character in this installment. His journey as both a warrior and a father adds a depth to his character that is truly remarkable, blending raw power with vulnerability in a way we’ve never seen before.

The visuals are absolutely stunning. Every environment, from the snow-capped mountains of Midgard to the otherworldly realms of Norse mythology, feels alive and meticulously crafted. The game is a visual feast that never fails to impress, whether you’re gazing at sprawling landscapes or battling through fire and ice.

Combat in God of War is nothing short of phenomenal. The Leviathan Axe is one of the most satisfying weapons I’ve ever wielded in a game. Throwing it, recalling it, and chaining attacks together feels fluid and impactful, and the depth of the combat system is perfectly balanced—simple to pick up, but difficult to master. Playing on the hardest difficulty turned every encounter into a calculated battle, and the Valkyrie bosses were a true test of patience and skill. They were challenging, no doubt, but overcoming them brought an incredible sense of accomplishment.

The story is where God of War truly shines. It’s not just entertaining but also educational, weaving elements of Norse mythology seamlessly into the narrative. Kratos’ relationship with Atreus is the heart of the game, and watching their bond evolve as they face trials together is both emotional and rewarding. It’s a powerful tale of redemption, responsibility, and the weight of the past, and it unfolds beautifully.

God of War (2018) manages to honor its roots while taking bold steps forward. It’s a game that hits all the marks—gorgeous visuals, engaging combat, and a deeply emotional story—and it never loses momentum. Playing on the hardest difficulty made it all the more memorable, as every victory felt earned, and every challenge added to the experience.

SSX 3

The Ultimate Snowboarding Experience

SSX 3 isn’t just a game—it’s an experience that captures the thrill, style, and sheer fun of snowboarding like nothing else. This game didn’t just entertain me; it inspired me to pick up a snowboard in real life and chase the exhilarating freedom it portrays so perfectly.

The characters in SSX 3 are some of the coolest I’ve seen in gaming. They have distinct personalities and styles that make them memorable and relatable. It’s easy to get attached, whether it’s rooting for your favorite rider or trying to emulate their tricks and swagger. The character designs and animations bring so much personality to the slopes, adding to the game’s charm.

For its time, the graphics were revolutionary. The seamless open-world mountain, with its snowy vistas, winding courses, and dynamic weather effects, feels alive and immersive. From carving down a sparkling glacier to leaping off massive cliffs, SSX 3 perfectly captures the vibe of an epic snowboarding adventure. Even today, the visuals hold up surprisingly well, thanks to the game’s vibrant art style and fluid animations.

The gameplay is where SSX 3 truly shines. It’s fast, smooth, and ridiculously fun. The trick system is intuitive but deep, letting you pull off insane combos with a bit of practice. Grinding rails, launching off ramps, and stringing together flips and spins feels incredibly satisfying. The flow of the game—especially in its open mountain design—is unlike anything else. You can ride uninterrupted from peak to base, an incredible feature that makes the game feel limitless.

What sets SSX 3 apart is the way it combines its gameplay, atmosphere, and soundtrack into an unforgettable experience. It’s not just about snowboarding; it’s about the vibe. The adrenaline-pumping music, the chill exploration, and the excitement of nailing tricks all come together to create something truly special.

This isn’t just one of the best snowboarding games—it’s one of the best games, period. Whether you’re chasing high scores, exploring the mountain, or just having fun, SSX 3 is a phenomenal ride from start to finish.
